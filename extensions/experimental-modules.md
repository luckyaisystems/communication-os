# Experimental Modules

The Experimental Modules directory contains high‑risk, high‑reward concepts that extend the Communication Operating System (OS) beyond its current architecture. These modules are prototypes, research ideas, or advanced systems that may eventually graduate into core modules or optimization layers.

Experimental modules are not guaranteed to be stable. They exist to explore new communication mechanics.

---

# Purpose

To provide a sandbox for:

- Prototype subsystems  
- High‑complexity heuristics  
- Advanced prediction engines  
- Non‑deterministic logic experiments  
- Future architecture layers  
- Concepts that require testing before integration  

This folder is the R&D lab of the Communication OS.

---

# Characteristics of Experimental Modules

Experimental modules typically:

- Introduce new logic not yet validated  
- Explore communication behaviors outside core lanes  
- Test new forms of drift detection  
- Prototype advanced cadence or tone systems  
- Experiment with multi‑layer pipelines  
- Extend telemetry beyond current capabilities  

They are **not** required to be stable, deterministic, or fully documented.

# Integration Rules

Experimental modules must:

- Never override core modules  
- Never break existing architecture  
- Remain isolated within `/extensions`  
- Document all experimental behavior  
- Update CROSS‑REFERENCES when relevant  
- Log major events in the iteration‑log  
- Be clearly marked as unstable  

Experimental modules may be removed, replaced, or rewritten at any time.

---

# Graduation Criteria

An experimental module may graduate into `/modules` if:

- It proves stable  
- It integrates cleanly with the compiler pipeline  
- It supports persona consistency  
- It improves clarity or cadence  
- It passes evaluation layer scoring  
- It aligns with OS principles in [PRINCIPLES](ca://s?q=Open_PRINCIPLES_file)  

Graduation is rare and requires maintainer approval.

---

# Contribution Guidelines

When adding new experimental modules:

- Clearly label them as experimental  
- Document purpose and risk  
- Provide examples  
- Update CHANGELOG  
- Maintain modularity  
- Ensure compatibility with telemetry and optimization passes
