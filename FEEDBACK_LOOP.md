# FEEDBACK LOOP

The Feedback Loop explains how real-world experience is transformed into durable engineering knowledge.
It is the core process that governs how every LuckyAISystems repository evolves over time.

This loop is grounded in reality as the source of truth.
AI, documentation, professors, manuals, mentors, and personal reasoning are contributors to analysis — not replacements for experience.

# Stage Breakdown

## Reality / Experience
Internship work, Seneca labs, conversations with technicians, debugging, equipment behavior, documentation — this is the raw material.

## Observation
A pattern, failure mode, anomaly, behavior, or question is noticed.

## Reflection
You consider why it matters and what it might imply.

## Question
You form a precise question that guides the next step.

## Research & Analysis
This may come from:
- documentation
- professors
- manuals
- mentors
- AI
- your own reasoning

AI is one contributor, not the center.

## Abstraction
You extract the underlying principle, model, or pattern.

## Verification
You check the abstraction against reality, documentation, or expert feedback.

## Artifact
You create something durable:
- diagram
- calculator
- write-up
- diagnostic tool
- decision tree
- failure audit

## Knowledge Base
The artifact becomes part of your engineering knowledge system.

## Future Observation
Your knowledge base improves what you notice next time — the loop grows, not closes.

---

# Purpose

The Feedback Loop explains how experience is transformed into durable engineering knowledge. Rather than creating projects in isolation, each artifact begins with a real observation, is refined through analysis and verification, and is published only after the underlying engineering concept is understood. Each completed artifact improves future observation by providing reusable models, documentation, and decision support.

# Notes

- This is a continuous learning cycle, not a closed loop.
- Each new experience changes the knowledge base.
- Each change in the knowledge base improves future observation.
- You never return to the same point — you return with more experience.
