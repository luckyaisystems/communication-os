# Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
The following methods define the repeatable procedures used throughout the Communication Operating System (OS). These SOPs operationalize the core principles and ensure consistent, modular, engineering-grade communication.

---

# 1. Communication Evaluation SOP
A structured process for evaluating any communication artifact.

## Step 1 — Define Context
Identify the topic, lane, tone, and constraints of the communication.

## Step 2 — Define Criteria
Select the relevant evaluation criteria:
- Context alignment
- Cadence discipline
- Sensory clarity
- Observational accuracy
- Modularity
- Tone control

## Step 3 — Assign Weights
Adjust importance based on context:
- Professional → clarity > sensory
- Casual → pacing > structure
- Technical → precision > tone

## Step 4 — Score Metrics
Use consistent scoring formats:
- 1–5 scale
- High/Medium/Low
- Pass/Fail

## Step 5 — Explain Reasoning
Provide a concise explanation for each score.

## Step 6 — Produce Final Evaluation
Combine weighted metrics into a final judgment.

---

# 2. Observation → Abstraction Workflow
A method for converting real communication cases into reusable principles.

## Step 1 — Observe
Capture the raw communication example.

## Step 2 — Extract
Identify the underlying pattern or principle.

## Step 3 — Stress-Test
Check the principle against counterexamples.

## Step 4 — Refine
Adjust the principle if weaknesses appear.

## Step 5 — Update SOP
Integrate the refined principle into the OS.

---

# 3. Cadence Mapping SOP
A procedure for maintaining stable pacing and tone.

## Step 1 — Identify Lane
Determine the current conversational lane:
- Sensory
- Informational
- Observational
- Technical

## Step 2 — Match Tone
Align tone with the lane:
- Neutral before personal
- Observational before interpretive

## Step 3 — Maintain Rhythm
Use controlled pacing:
- Avoid spikes
- Avoid premature module jumps
- Keep statements single-purpose

## Step 4 — Anchor Sensory Detail
Use scenery, food, atmosphere, or environment to stabilize tone.

---

# 4. Modularity SOP
Ensures communication remains clean and structured.

## Step 1 — Single Purpose
Each message should serve one clear function.

## Step 2 — Module Separation
If multiple ideas are required, separate them into distinct sentences.

## Step 3 — Avoid Overloading
Do not mix emotional, temporal, and sensory modules unless context supports it.

---

# 5. Context Drift Detection SOP
A method for identifying when communication shifts lanes prematurely.

## Step 1 — Detect New Modules
Identify if the message introduces:
- Future planning
- Emotional implication
- Personal framing
- New conceptual lanes

## Step 2 — Compare to Context
Check if the new module aligns with the established lane.

## Step 3 — Flag Drift
If misaligned, mark the message as a pacing spike.

## Step 4 — Stabilize
Rewrite using sensory anchors or lane-consistent observations.

---

# Purpose of These Methods
These SOPs transform communication from instinct-driven to system-driven.  
They ensure clarity, consistency, and engineering-grade structure across all communication artifacts within the OS.
