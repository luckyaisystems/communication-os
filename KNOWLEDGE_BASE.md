# KNOWLEDGE BASE

The Knowledge Base is the collection of durable engineering insights, artifacts, and
models created through the Feedback Loop. It represents what survives after experience
has been observed, analyzed, abstracted, verified, and documented.

Unlike Infrastructure, which changes slowly, the Knowledge Base grows continuously.
Every internship observation, Seneca lab, engineering conversation, or debugging
session has the potential to become new knowledge.

The Knowledge Base is not a single repository. It is the interconnected set of all
artifacts across LuckyAISystems.

---

# What Counts as Knowledge

Knowledge is any artifact that captures an engineering concept, pattern, or insight
in a durable, reusable form. Examples include:

- Failure analyses
- Engineering notes
- Diagnostic procedures
- Visual diagrams
- Calculators and estimators
- Decision trees
- System models
- Write-ups and explanations
- Observational logs
- Abstraction summaries

Knowledge must be grounded in real experience or verified engineering principles.
It is not speculative. It is not hypothetical. It is earned.

---

# How Knowledge Is Created

Knowledge is produced through the Feedback Loop:

Reality / Experience
        ↓
Observation
        ↓
Reflection
        ↓
Question
        ↓
Research & Analysis
        ↓
Abstraction
        ↓
Verification
        ↓
Artifact
        ↓
Knowledge Base
        ↓
Future Observation

Each artifact becomes part of the Knowledge Base only after it has been verified
against reality, documentation, or expert feedback.

---

# Characteristics of Strong Knowledge

Strong knowledge has the following traits:

## Grounded
It originates from real experience, equipment behavior, documentation, or verified
engineering principles.

## Durable
It remains useful over time and can be applied to multiple situations.

## Modular
It can be referenced, linked, or reused across different repositories.

## Clear
It is documented in a way that others can understand without prior context.

## Connected
It links back to the Infrastructure Layer and integrates with other artifacts.

---

# How Knowledge Connects Across Repositories

The Knowledge Base is distributed across multiple repositories, but unified through:

- consistent documentation standards
- shared communication conventions
- cross-references
- diagrams
- decision trees
- engineering journals
- repository templates

This creates a coherent ecosystem where each artifact strengthens the others.

---

# Growth Over Time

The Knowledge Base grows through:

- internship observations
- Seneca coursework
- engineering conversations
- equipment troubleshooting
- reading manuals and documentation
- verified abstractions
- new diagrams and models
- improved decision frameworks

Each new artifact improves future observation, creating a continuous learning cycle.
